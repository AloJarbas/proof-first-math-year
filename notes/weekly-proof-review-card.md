# Weekly proof review card

The proof journal is where mistakes become visible.
This card is where the next week gets corrected.

Use it once per week, after the individual journal entries are done.

## The four checks that matter

### 1. Retrieval check
Do not only reread old proofs.

Pick **2-3 finished proofs** from earlier in the week and try to reconstruct them from memory:

- one the next day,
- one after 3-4 days,
- one at the end of the week.

If the reconstruction collapses, count that as evidence. The proof was not learned yet.

### 2. Worked-example check
Pick **one solved example** from the text or notes and audit it actively:

- Why was this proof method chosen?
- Which definition did the real work?
- Which step would break first if the hypotheses changed?
- Can I hide the last third and finish it myself?

The point is not to admire clean solutions. It is to turn them into usable moves.

### 3. Interleaving check
Run **one mixed block** instead of one perfectly themed block.

A good mixed set might contain:

- one direct proof,
- one contradiction or contrapositive proof,
- one counterexample task,
- one definition-unpacking exercise,
- one induction problem.

This forces the real skill: recognizing what kind of problem is in front of you.

### 4. Load check
Classify the week honestly:

- **green** — proof work is holding; a secondary stream may stay active
- **yellow** — proof work is shaky; keep secondary streams light
- **red** — proof work is fragmenting; narrow the load next week

## How to use the result

If the week is **green**:
- keep calculus or discrete math active,
- but do not expand both at once unless proof quality is still steady.

If the week is **yellow**:
- keep proof primary,
- reduce secondary intensity,
- choose one error tag for deliberate repair.

If the week is **red**:
- narrow the plan immediately,
- cut optional side material,
- focus on definitions, short proofs, and rewrites from memory.

Narrowing is not failure.
It is how the proof-first plan stays honest.

## Why this card exists

The earlier notes already had the right bones:

- proof journal,
- error taxonomy,
- entry gates,
- weekly load adjustment.

This card tightens the loop by adding three things that are easy to skip unless they are named explicitly:

- spaced retrieval,
- active study of worked examples,
- interleaved mixed practice.

That gives the week a better chance of improving actual proof control instead of only increasing page count.
